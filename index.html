About

STARS AT TEMPLE

STARS Computing Corps at Temple is a student run organization with a mission to engage primary and secondary students throughout the Philadelphia School District in Computer Science related studies.

Beginning with the Philadelphia Military Academy, our student volunteers visit the classroom setting to assist and mentor high school students in their AP Computer Science class. With free, interative workshops and guest speaker events, we hope to inspire or spark interest in technology. We hope to highlight the opportunities and possibilities that computer science has to offer.

To learn more details about us, please visit "Our Efforts" section and explore different ways to get involved.

Technical Workshops

The Technical Workshop Committee's mission is to organize computer science related workshops for high school students. Workshops are held at Temple University, allowing students the opportunity to visit the campus and experience student life. They will be under the instruction of dedicated undergraduates and graduate students who are excited to share their knowledge and passion for different computer science topics. Teaching Assistants

STARS at Temple is dedicated to broadening awareness of the possibilities of technology and computer science to younger students. One way to reach the youth is through their schools. STARS at Temple has a group of volunteers dedicated to visiting classrooms to assist in students' engagement of computer science or other related topics. As of today, we are volunteering at the Philadelphia Military Academy, providing support and mentorship to students in their AP Computer Science course. Guest Speaker

The Guest Speaker Event is a 2-hour after school event held at Temple University's main campus. This event is open to local high school students who are interested in pursuing a career in Computer Science related fields or to students who are just curious about this area of study. During this event, 2-4 professionals are invited to speak about various aspects of their career including their roles, what they enjoy, how to get involved, their work environment, etc. Students are free to ask questions and network with these guests as this event is designed to be more discussion-based rather than lecture-based.
Snacks and refreshments are provided!
